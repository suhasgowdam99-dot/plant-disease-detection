Problem Statement
The gap between gardening and smart care
Home gardeners and bonsai enthusiasts lose precious plants every year — not because they don't care, but because they lack the right tools to act at the right time.
⚠️ Core Problem

Plant diseases spread silently — by the time they're visible, it's often too late

Traditional gardening relies entirely on manual visual inspection. This means disease is only noticed when it has already damaged a significant portion of the plant. Worse, when gardeners do react, they resort to full-plant chemical spraying — harming healthy tissue, wasting pesticide, and creating chemical runoff that damages surrounding soil. There is no awareness of environmental conditions like humidity or soil moisture that directly trigger disease outbreaks. The result: beautiful and expensive plants — especially bonsai — are lost to problems that could have been caught and treated precisely, weeks earlier.

⚠️

Late detection

Diseases are spotted too late. By the time symptoms appear, the plant is already heavily infected — costly plants like bonsai may be lost entirely.

💧

Blanket spraying

Spraying the entire plant wastes chemicals, harms healthy roots and leaves, and is especially dangerous for delicate ornamental plants.

📊

No environmental data

Without real-time temperature, humidity, and moisture monitoring, gardeners can't predict or prevent disease — they only react after damage is done.

Proposed Solution
How the system works — step by step
A closed-loop smart system that watches, detects, decides, and acts — automatically and precisely.
💡 Core Idea

Combine AI vision + IoT sensors + automated spraying into one smart loop

Instead of waiting for visible damage, the system continuously watches the plant's environment and appearance. When disease is detected, it precisely targets only the affected area — no guesswork, no waste.

📱
STEP 01

Capture a photo of the plant

The gardener points their smartphone at the plant. The app's camera captures a clear image of the leaves and stem. No special equipment needed — just a regular phone camera.

Smartphone CameraMobile App
🔍
STEP 02

AI analyses the image for disease

The TensorFlow.js model on the phone inspects the photo. It identifies the disease type (fungal, bacterial, etc.), marks the infected region, and estimates how much of the plant is affected.

TensorFlow.jsDisease ClassificationDamage % Estimate
🌡️
STEP 03

ESP32 checks environmental conditions

Before doing anything, the ESP32 reads the DHT22 sensor (temperature + humidity) and the soil moisture sensor. If conditions are already too wet or too hot, spraying is paused to avoid making things worse.

DHT22 SensorSoil MoistureCondition Check
🧠
STEP 04

ESP32 decides — spray or skip?

The ESP32 acts as the brain. It combines the AI diagnosis from the phone with the live sensor data. If both signals say "treat now," it triggers the pump for exactly the right duration — targeting only the infected zone.

ESP32 ControllerSmart Decision LogicPWM Pump Control
🔄
STEP 05

Log, learn, and get smarter

Every detection and spray event is recorded in the app. Over time, this history helps the AI become more accurate for your specific plant type, climate, and garden conditions — the system keeps improving with every use.
